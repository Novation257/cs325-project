NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3070 Poised For Value And Performance-Per-Watt Leadership

Oct 27, 2020, 09:01am EDT

Usually, when it comes to PC gaming graphics cards, you can’t have your cake and eat it too. Typically, dramatically higher levels of performance command either higher power consumption or higher price points, and sometimes it’s actually both. However, with the launch of the new GeForce RTX 3070 Founder’s Edition, a new $499 upper-midrange offering direct from NVIDIA, the company seems focused on breaking through the perf-per-watt and perf-per-dollar watermarks it previous set with the launch of its Turing GPU architecture and GeForce RTX 20 series of cards. We’ve already seen what NVIDIA’s new Ampere GPU architecture can do for ultra-high-end gaming with the launch of the GeForce RTX 3080 and GeForce RTX 3090. However, today marks the company’s official appeal to the mainstream and, from what I’ve seen so far, the value proposition on a number of fronts is strong.
Architecturally, NVIDIA’s new GeForce RTX 3070 series of cards is a case study in silicon optimization. Compared to the previous generation RTX 2070, the new RTX 3070 offers significantly more resources with over twice the number of CUDA cores, as well as 10 more second gen RT (ray tracing) cores, 40 more texture units and 32 more ROPs (Render Output Units). Tensor cores for NVIDIA DLSS (Deep Learning Super Sampling) and machine learning (AI) workloads are scaled back, to 184 (versus 288 in the previous gen) but NVIDIA notes Ampere’s RT cores are two times more powerful than the previous generation. The RTX 3070 Founder’s Edition card’s power requirements scale up an additional 35 Watts, but available memory bandwidth and total available VRAM memory are essentially unchanged versus the previous generation. The card’s GPU boost clock is also only slightly higher at 1725MHz (versus 1710MHz in the previous gen RTX 2070), and its GDDR6 memory clock (7Ghz) remains the same as well.
Regardless, the performance, power and acoustic profile NVIDIA is able to strike with this new gaming GPU sets new levels of efficiency. NVIDIA claims its GeForce RTX 3070, at $499, will offer roughly equivalent performance to its previous generation GeForce RTX 2080 Ti that currently retails for no less than $1200, and occasionally best it by a hair in certain workloads. In my personal testing, and in Marco Chiappetta’s deep dive at HotHardware.com, that’s exactly what we found.
NVIDIA’s new GeForce RTX 3070 was consistently within approximately 2 – 8% of the significantly more expensive GeForce RTX 2080 Ti’s performance levels. This translates to 4K resolutions with Ultra image quality settings in almost any triple-A game title, at 50 – 60 FPS or higher, no matter what you throw at it.  If you tack on ray tracing with NVIDIA RTX and DLSS enabled, the gap between the RTX 3070 and RTX 2080 Ti tends to shrink even further.
What’s perhaps more impressive for me though, is the RTX 3070’s power consumption that shows the card sips roughly 60 Watts less power under load while gaming, versus a GeForce RTX 2080 Ti. In fact, we measured the GeForce RTX 3070 only consuming approximately 13 Watts more under load (about 5% higher) than a previous generation GeForce RTX 2070 Super, though it offers approximately 25 – 30+% better performance.
So, in short, with the RTX 3070 we’re talking roughly a 25 - 30% performance gain (or more), at a lower price point than NVIDIA’s previous generation GeForce RTX 2070 Super card, while consuming only 5% more power. Or from a different perspective, with the RTX 3070, you’re looking at similar performance to NVIDIA’s previous generation, high-end $1200 RTX 2080 TI GPU, at a fraction of the price, and with roughly 25% less power consumption.
