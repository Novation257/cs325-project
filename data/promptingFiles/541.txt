NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3080 Ti Lights-Up Gaming Benchmarks And Turns-Up Heat On AMD

Jun 02, 2021, 09:01am EDT

If you’re a PC gaming enthusiast or are even just tracking NVIDIA for its dynamic tech sector impact in computing, you’ve likely been watching the company’s ramp-up to Computex 2021, which is taking place this week virtually as Taiwan’s biggest tech trade show. Though there have been numerous NVIDIA announcements this week, from enterprise AI technologies to big PC game title collaboration, perhaps none have been so highly anticipated as the launch of the NVIDIA’s next generation flagship GeForce RTX 3080 Ti PC gaming GPU, that the company claims is 1.5x faster than its previous generation GeForce RTX 2080 Ti card. Today NVIDIA is officially lifting the veil on details and deep dive reviews of this latest high-end PC graphics offering that slots in at an $1199 price point, whereas its current ultra-high end GeForce RTX 3090 product resides at $1499.
Of course, in the current chip shortage (especially GPUs), and associated skyrocketed pricing at retail, these MSRP price points are the only relative gauges we have to go by, though suffice it to say that historically GeForce Ti cards are mid-life kicker sorts of products, targeted at elevating performance and feature sets versus competitive offerings from AMD, and maybe Intel in the not-so distant future.
To that end, the GeForce RTX 3080 Ti delivers the goods specs-wise, with a full 10240 CUDA cores versus the RTX 3080’s 8704 cores, along with 12GB of GDDR6X memory versus the RTX 3080’s 10GB complement. The GeForce RTX 3080 Ti’s memory also sits on a wider 384-bit GDDR6 memory interface (320-bit for RTX 3080) and delivers a scorching 912GB/s of memory bandwidth versus 760GB/sec with a standard GeForce RTX 3080. And versus NVIDIA’s previous generation GeForce RTX 2080 Ti Founders Edition card, other resources (and board power to a lesser degree) have scaled dramatically. For those of you that like to pore over speeds and feeds, take a look…
Physically, you’d be hard-pressed to tell the difference between the new GeForce RTX 3080 Ti Founders Edition and the current-gen GeForce RTX 3080. Though TGP (Total Graphics, or board, Power) has increased to 350 Watts, versus 320 Watts for an RTX 3080, the GeForce RTX 3080 Ti looks identical, save for its moniker branding. It even weighs the same as a standard RTX 3080 at a shade under 4 lbs. However, all told, the GeForce RTX 3080 Ti is really more closely aligned to NVIDIA’s beastly (and much larger) GeForce RTX 3090 (with 24GB of GDDR6X), only with half the memory and a few CUDA cores disabled in the 3080 Ti (10240 vs 10496 in the 3090) as well.
Regardless, the new GeForce RTX 3080 Ti’s thermal solution is cool, collected and quiet under pressure, just like NVIDIA’s standard RTX 3080. In fact, in testing at HotHardware.com, we’ve found it to be quieter than its closest rival from AMD, the Radeon RX 6900 XT, though it does consume about the same power under load. In short, NVIDIA’s new GeForce RTX 3080 Ti is generally faster than a Radeon RX 6900 XT, the fastest card AMD currently has to offer at a (target) $999 MSRP.
In traditional rasterized game engines (no ray tracing) its margin of victory is slim over AMD’s offering, but the new 3080 Ti is also only a hair behind a more expensive GeForce RTX 3090. As such, the GeForce RTX 3080 Ti is a better buy for enthusiast gamers, though, for content creation and workstation professional types, the RTX 3090’s extra 12GB GDDR6X may be critical differentiator. However, flip on ray tracing and the NVIDIA versus AMD comparison looks dramatically different.
Even at 4K resolutions with Ultra quality presets and no DLSS (Deep Learning Super Sampling) turned on, the GeForce RTX 3080 Ti can maintain playable frame rates in at taxing, ray tracing-enhanced titles like Metro Exodus. And as you can see here, it can be as much 40% faster than a Radeon RX 6900 XT when ray traced game engine workloads are involved. This is a testament to NVIDIA’s second generation RT engines on board its RTX 30 series GPUs, while AMD has only had this current generation of Radeons (and its console counterparts) to cut its teeth on ray tracing. Either way, be sure to check out HotHardware’s full deep dive review with lots more GeForce RTX 3080 Ti benchmark data.
Generally speaking, NVIDIA’s new GeForce RTX 3080 Ti brings RTX 3090-class performance for PC gamers at $300 less, as long as you can actually acquire one at retail for that price, so it’s a clear win. However, whether or not your feel it’s a win versus AMD’s less expensive Radeon RX 6900 XT will likely depend on your priorities around the 3080 Ti’s obvious ray tracing prowess, as well as the game titles that are most important to you. By the way, NVIDIA also just announced that Doom Eternal is inbound with ray tracing, and I’m really looking forward to checking that gorgeous game engine out with new levels of visual fidelity. Obviously, as ray tracing is bound to become even more impactful in next generation cutting-edge game engines, NVIDIA’s significant lead in this area could result in a more future-proof product as well. NVIDIA notes GeForce RTX 3080 Ti Founders Edition and 3rd party board partner cards will be available in retail starting tomorrow.
Regardless, the execution machine that is NVIDIA rolls on today with the introduction of the new GeForce RTX 3080 Ti, and in the coming weeks the $599 GeForce RTX 3070 Ti as well. The latter product will likely make a bigger splash at its much more palatable mainstream price point. So stay tuned for that, as midrange graphics card options are poised to get a lot more interesting.
