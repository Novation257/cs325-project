Nvidia RTX 3080 Ti Vs RTX 3080, RTX 3090 And RX 6900 XT: Which should You Buy?

Jun 02, 2021, 08:59am EDT

Nvidia (NASDAQ:NVDA) launched its RTX 3080 Ti as well as the RTX 3070 Ti yesterday, but it's the former that has seen it's performance embargo lift today, allowing us to offer full reviews of the card and reveal just how fast it is in games.
You can also see my RTX 3070 Ti review:
Also in question is where this new RTX 3000-series sits in the overall scheme of things. Is it an RTX 3080 replacement? Does it outperform the RTX 3090 and AMD RX 6900 XT? Let's find out.
To start with, the specifications and price point at the RTX 3080 Ti sitting much closer to the RTX 3090. Only the memory amount stands as a significant trump card with the RTX 3090, with more modest advantages in boost frequency, CUDA cores, Tensor cores and RT cores for example. The price, too, is also close, with just $300 separating them, so even if the RTX 3080 Ti offers similar performance, any RTX 3090 owners out there can probably breath a sigh of relief. Had the RTX 3080 Ti landed with an MSRP of $900, that would have been a different story.
The RTX 3080 Ti and RTX 3080 Founders Edition cards, which I'm reviewing, are physically identical in terms of the design and size of the familiar cooler, although with an extra 30W of board power to contend with - the same as the RTX 3090 - the RTX 3080 Ti may well need to spin up its fans more regularly to deal with the heat compared to the RTX 3080.
I've used the latest drivers from Nvidia and AMD as well as a fully up-to-date version of Windows 10 as of 31st May 2021. As we're dealing with high-end cards in this review, my tests focus on 2,560 x 1,440 and 4K resolutions. I've tested games with DLSS and ray tracing enabled, but also disabled on occasions - I'm very much in favor of using these features where they're available, but can appreciate not all will want to. As we're dealing with ultra high-end cards here, I've only included the RX 6900 XT to save time, but will be including the RX 6800 XT in my RTX 3070 Ti review.
My hardware includes a water-cooled BarrowCH Rhopilema water-cooled test bench, 16GB Corsair Vengeance RGB Pro 3,466MHz memory, Intel Core i9-11900K, an Intel 760p M.2 SSD, Corsair RM850i PSU and MSI MEG Z590 Ace motherboard. I've carried out all tests with resizable BAR/Smart Access Memory support enabled.
Below we start with Watch Dogs: Legion and with Ray Tracing enabled but DLSS disabled, the RTX 3080 Ti came within spitting distance of the RTX 3090 and sits almost exactly where you'd expect it to in terms of price compared to the RTX 3080, with a sizable lead over it.
Dialling up to 4K and the  RTX 3080 Ti slipped back from the RTX 3090, but again held a commanding lead over the RTX 3080, while the RX 6900 XT really doesn't seem to like this game with ray tracing enabled.
With DLSS enabled, the Nvidia cards unsurprisingly shoot ahead and once again the  RTX 3080 Ti is level pegging with the RTX 3090 and offering a decent gain over the RTX 3080.
Dirt 5 below now features ray tracing support and here the RX 6900 XT performs much better, sitting between the RTX 3080 and  RTX 3080 Ti. Again, though, the RTX 3090 still has the edge, while the RTX 3080 is significantly slower at 1440p.
Switching up to 4K and and the  RTX 3080 Ti falls back from the RTX 3090, but maintains its lead over the RTX 3080 with a minimum 99th percentile of 70fps over 60fps.
Microsoft Flight Simulator is a tough game to play and here I've used an external view at low altitude taking off from a major UK airport - usually where I see the lowest frame rates. At 1440p, the RX 6900 XT was king with a minimum 99th percentile of 38fps followed closely by the  RTX 3080 Ti and RTX 3090 and the RTX 3080 in a distant fourth.
Dialling up to 4K and the RTX 3090 is the card to have with the RX 6900 XT falling away to sit next to the RTX 3080. The  RTX 3080 Ti offered a higher average frame rate than the RTX 3080 here.
Wolfenstein is a very easy game to play, but plenty of load put on the GPU. Here, the  RTX 3080 Ti matched the RTX 3090. While the other cards were a lot slower, at these frame rates it could be down to the CPU being the bottleneck - that's certainly what the results at 4K seem to suggest below - but things don't get much faster than the Core i9-11900K so there's not much you can do about that.
Putting more load on the GPU sees the two separate and the RTX 3090 leap ahead and once again the  RTX 3080 Ti is performing roughly how you'd expect. The RX 6900 XT, meanwhile, is a long way from matching the  RTX 3080 Ti, even with DLSS and ray tracing disabled
Adding DLSS and ray tracing into the equation means dropping the RX 6900 XT from the graphs as it doesn't support ray tracing in this title. Once again the  RTX 3080 Ti is sitting where it should, closer to the RTX 3090 than RTX 3080.
The new enhanced edition of Metro Exodus below means I can justify using this excellent benchmark again and at 4K with DLSS disabled, the RTX 3080 Ti all but matched the RTX 3090 while offering a sizeable lead over the RTX 3080 and RX 6900 XT.
Adding DLSS into the mix saw the RTX 3090 edge out a small lead while the RTX 3080 Ti had less of a lead over the RTX 3080, with the RX 6900 XT showing why AMD needs to get its DLSS equivalent - FidelityFX out the door.
At 4K the difference between AMD and Nvidia was even more stark with DLSS enabled while the RTX 3080 Ti actually bettered the RTX 3090's average frame rate and again managing a decent lead over the RTX 3080.
If there's one area AMD has a commanding lead, it's power consumption with the RX 6900 XT certainly far more appealing than the RTX 3090 and RTX 3080 Ti, both of which saw the system draw well over 500W. The RTX 3080 Ti also drew noticeably more power than the RTX 3080, confirming my thoughts that this could be a hotter/noisier card in some situations. It did seem to be a little louder in games, but the peak core temperature was only one degree higher in my power consumption stress test.
The elephant in the room is availability and pricing. None of the above matters if you can't buy the hardware or it's retailing for double the MSRP. The RTX 3080 Ti should be far less attractive to miners - I measured a hashrate of around 60MH/sec mining Ethereum, and that makes it the least attractive RTX 3000-series card by far to miners in terms of power efficiency. However, it's not just mining that's causing the crazy price and supply situation, but the microchip shortage too.
Time will tell, but ultimately we're not looking at an RTX 3080 replacement here anyway. It's too expensive for that and as Nvidia is ushering in hashrate-limited versions of the RTX 3080, it's not a card that's specifically aiming at being less attractive to miners either. That said, as the RTX 3090 is seemingly not going to be receiving the same light hashrate treatment as cheaper cards, the RTX 3080 Ti might be a more readily available card as a result, at least as far as the impact of the RTX 3090 being snapped up by miners goes.
Gaming performance is why we're here, though, and overall the RTX 3080 Ti sits exactly where it should - close to the RTX 3090, but usually offering a sizeable lead over the RTX 3080. Is it a 3090 killer for half the price? No. Nvidia hasn't really pulled the rug from under RTX 3090 owners here at all. It's simply filling the void between the RTX 3090 and 3080 both in terms of price and performance, without toppling the RTX 3090, which is still the ultimate gaming card, especially at 4K.
Even without ray tracing and DLSS enabled, though, it's still a very powerful GPU, but that big elephant is still sitting there in the room. Just whether it will be available and anywhere near its MSRP is an entirely different matter and for that reason, drawing firmer conclusions, even against the RX 6900 XT, which it beat convincingly in many tests, is rather difficult. The RTX 3070 Ti, might appeal more since it's far cheaper, but Nvidia now has two monstrously powerful $1,000+ graphics cards. The only question is, can you actually buy either of them, and if you can afford to, would you really want to save the cash over the RTX 3090?
