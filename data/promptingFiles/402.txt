Deja Vu? Nvidia Is 2024's Best Performing Big Stock So Far

Jan 09, 2024, 04:09pm EST

Nvidia stock rose to a fresh all-time high Tuesday, as the artificial intelligence heavyweight continues to provide market-beating gains for investors in the new year.
Shares of Nvidia rose 1.7% to $531.40, setting a new record closing share price.
Nvidia’s rally this week comes as the company unveiled new video game-focused AI offerings and Oppenheimer analyst Rick Schafer named Nvidia as one of his top stock picks for semiconductor chip companies, but largely appears to be a continuation of the positive sentiments accompanying the stock for 2023, when Nvidia was by far the best-performing stock listed on the S&P 500.
Up almost 9% so far this year, shares of Nvidia have again far outperformed the limping broader market, with the S&P and tech-heavy Nasdaq indexes each down.
Nvidia is the S&P’s fourth-best-performing stock through the first six trading sessions and by far the biggest gainer among companies with a market capitalization over $50 billion.
Notably, Nvidia shares have moved against an otherwise down stretch for big tech stocks, as the other six largest tech companies by market capitalization—Apple, Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta and Tesla—have posted an average return of -1% so far this year.
