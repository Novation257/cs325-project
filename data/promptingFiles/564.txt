The Nvidia GeForce RTX 3070 – A Good GPU Card At A Great Price

Nov 06, 2020, 11:07am EST

Earlier this month Tirias Research tested the top end of Nvidia’s new dynamic duo the GeForce RTX 3080 Founder Edition and we were thoroughly impressed, Now we’ve been given the opportunity to review it’s more cost sensitive sister - the GeForce RTX 3070. While there is a noticeable difference in the cards both physically and performance wise, it’s a very impressive card for just $499.
The first thing you notice about the 3070 is that it is physically smaller than the 3080 and lacks some of the nickel accents that wrap around the 3080. It still has a dual fan cooling design but in a more traditional configuration. Inside the case, the 3070 is based on the same Ampere architecture and 8nm manufacturing process as the 3080, but the 3070 has a reduced core count (CUDA cores, texture units, tensor cores and ray tracing cores) and memory is reduced from 10GB of GDDR6X in the 3080 to 8GB of GDDR6 in the 3070. However, this also results in a 100W power reduction with the 3070 rated at just 220W. This was very noticeable in testing because the fan noise was quieter - more in line with the previous GeForce RTX 2070 Super but with much higher performance. And all that comes with Nvidia’s ray tracing and Deep Learning Super-Sampling (DLSS) technology.
For testing, we used the same 2nd-gen AMD Threadripper system used to test the 3080 and limited the tests to GPU-centric benchmarks. In all the graphics benchmarks, the delta between the 3080 and the 3070 was less than 10%. The delta was 9.9% in 3D Mark Time Spy, 8.92% using PassMark in 3D graphics, 3.4% using Unigine Superposition at 1080p. The largest gaps in performance come at 4K and in GPU compute. The delta between the two cards was 25% using Unigine Superposition at 4K and 27.6% using Geekbench for OpenCL. However, the compute delta is likely much lower using Nvidia’s CUDA for GPU compute.
For gaming, we stuck to the games supporting ray tracing, including Battlefield V, Call of Duty Modern Warfare, Fortnite, and Minecraft. When it came to gaming, the RTX 3070 had great resolution and performance at 1080P and 1440P with a slight performance degradation at 4K, but still very playable.
The competitive gamer or high-end creator will still likely opt for the GeForce RTX 3080 or RTX 3090. At just $499, 29% less than the 3080, but with less than a 10% difference in 1080P and 1440P gaming performance, the 3070 is the perfect solution for the more mainstream gamer. In my previous article I argued that it may be better to upgrade your PC before buying a new PC console and I’m sticking to that sentiment. Both the RTX 3080 and RTX 3070 are impressive cards at reasonable prices. And apparently everyone else thinks so too because the orders are selling out as fast as Nvidia can make them.
