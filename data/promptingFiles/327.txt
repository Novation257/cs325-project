Nvidia—World’s Most Valuable Company—Rallies Another 3% As $4 Trillion Valuation In Sight

Jun 20, 2024, 09:43am EDT

Shares of the newly minted most valuable company in the world, Nvidia, rallied yet again Thursday, as predictions run wild of just how high the exponential growth stock, which has accounted for a major chunk of the record-setting stock market milestones, can go.
Nvidia’s stock opened up more than 3% at almost $140 per share, casually tacking on another $100 billion in market capitalization.
Now worth $3.44 trillion, Nvidia extended its lead as the biggest firm by market value over Microsoft ($3.29 trillion) and Apple ($3.26 trillion).
Nvidia boosted stock indexes to fresh all-time highs, propelling the S&P 500 to a new apex of 5,499.99, with the benchmark S&P’s performance closely linked to that of the trio, which account for more than 20% of the index’s total market cap.
As investors seemingly can’t get enough of big tech companies poised to profit off of the explosion in interest in generative artificial intelligence, “the race” to be the world’s first ever $4 trillion is now “front and center,” Wedbush analysts led by Dan Ives proclaimed in a Thursday note to clients, swatting away more conservative, fundamental-focused investors trepidation as not seeing the “forest through the trees on the AI revolution.”
And why stop at $4 trillion? Rosenblatt analyst Hans Mosesmann upped his price target for Nvidia to $200 per share Tuesday, indicating he anticipates the AI chip architect will have a $4.9 trillion market cap within the next 12 months, quite the turn for a company that only surpassed a $1 trillion valuation last May.

Nvidia remains very popular on Wall Street even after its stock price has risen by more than 800% over the past 18 months, but its ascent still flashes some signs of concern. Nvidia trades today at 79 times its trailing 12-month revenues and 42 times its trailing 12-month profits, fetching a price-to-sales ratio about 15 times richer than the median S&P component and a price-to-earnings ratio of about three times higher than the typical S&P stock. Basically, Nvidia’s valuation relies upon the AI golden child rapidly expanding earnings in coming quarters, which is in line with most analysts’ expectations, but still requires investors to take a leap of faith.
