Nvidia’s Record Rally Makes CEO Jensen Huang $10 Billion Richer

Feb 22, 2024, 11:21am EST

Nvidia’s historic stock market gains Thursday made the Silicon Valley titan’s billionaire CEO Jensen Huang a much richer man.
Huang, who owns about 3.5% of shares in the company he cofounded in 1993, became $9.5 billion richer Thursday as Nvidia’s stock soared by 16% following bumper earnings.
The 61-year-old Huang was by far the biggest winner Thursday of any billionaire on Forbes’ real-time rankings of the world’s richest people, becoming the 21st-wealthiest person with a $69.3 billion fortune.
Huang’s net worth has taken off as Nvidia stock skyrocketed amid the artificial intelligence boom; Huang’s fortune Thursday was 17 times higher than it was as of Forbes’ 2019 billionaires list, when it sat at a comparatively paltry $3.8 billion.
Nvidia’s second- and third-largest individual shareholders, long-time board members Tench Coxe and Mark Stevens, also enjoyed pretty paper gains Thursday, as Coxe’s net worth jumped by $480 million to $4.2 billion and Stevens’ fortune rose by $250 million to $6.3 billion.
