AMD Radeon RX 6700 XT Vs Nvidia RTX 3060 Ti And 3070: Which Should You Buy?

Mar 17, 2021, 08:59am EDT

Today we're finally able to lift the lid on performance numbers for AMD's RX 6700XT - the company's new mid-range graphics card that's set to retail for around $479. Of course, while AMD's Radeon RX 6000-series has been generally well-received and is a welcome return to the high-end market of PC gaming, availability of its cards, as with a lot of tech items at the moment, has been a real issue.
Whether the RX 6700 XT will be available in quantity or even anywhere near it's RRP remains to be seen, but there's not much we can do about that right now. At $479, it's a little cheaper than launch RRP of the RTX 3070 at $499, so what we're looking for here is a card that keeps up with the RTX 3070 and is significantly faster than the RTX 3060 Ti. Let's take a look at its performance.
I used AMD's 20.50 release driver for the RX 6700 XT testing along with a fully up-to-date version of Windows 10. The benchmarks span 1080p to 4K, but not all graphics cards are tested at all resolutions. For example, the RTX 3060 isn't tested at 4K since you'd be well-advised to opt for a more powerful card if that's your chosen resolution. The RX 6700 XT is a mid-range card that's capable of 4K in some games so I've included results at 4K in all my game tests. In addition, I've also included results for ray tracing, DLSS and Smart Access Memory in certain titles to see the impact these features make.
The test system used an MSI MEG Z490 Ace, Intel Core i9-10900K, 16GB 3466MHz Corsair memory and an Intel 760p SSD along with a custom watercooling system.
In Metro Exodus at 1080p with ray tracing and DLSS enabled, the 6700 XT lags behind the RTX 3060 Ti, which is clearly not ideal given the latter has a lower price in normal times, but this is thanks to DLSS, which isn't available in all games and AMD will have its own version of the technology in due course.
The same was true at 1440p with the 6700 XT sitting some way behind the RTX 3060 Ti. Now less see what happens if we turn off ray tracing and DLSS.
Below you can see that without the benefit of DLSS, the RX 6700 XT has a similar 99th percentile to the RTX 3070, although the latter enjoys a noticeably higher average frame rate. It's also worth noting the proximity of the RTX 3060 Ti here too - it's not far behind either the RTX 3070 or RX 6700 XT.
It's no surprise to see the result at 4K re-enabling DLSS and ray tracing, with the RX 6700 XT sitting below the RX 6800. The trouble here is that if you're prepared to use DLSS, the RTX 3060 Ti is far cheaper (without a pandemic and cryptocurrency mining surge) and does manage playable frame rates at 4K.
Switch that advantage off and the two cards are actually quite similar and the RTX 3070 is again a fair way ahead of the RX 6700 XT in Metro Exodus at 4K.
For some reason at 1080p AMD cards really don't perform that well against their green counterparts and even the RX 6800 failed to topple the RTX 3060 Ti.
At 1440p, order is restored and the RX 6700 XT managed to beat the RTX 3070's minimum 99th percentile, but the latter had a higher average frame rate. Again, though, the RTX 3060 Ti wasn't far behind at all.
At 4K things lean more to AMD's favor, with the RX 6700 XT snapping at the heels of the RTX 3070 and sitting a long way ahead of the RTX 3060 Ti.
Next up is Borderlands 3 and the RX 6700 XT doesn't really offer a compelling reason to chose it over the RTX 3060 Ti at 1080p, with the two fairly evenly matched.
Upping the resolution to 1440p and the situation is transformed, again matching the more expensive RTX 3070 - great news for AMD and Borderlands fans.
The game was plenty smooth enough at 4K and once again, the RX 6700 XT sits where it should given the price difference between it and the RTX 3070 - just behind and noticeably faster than the RTX 3060 Ti.
AMD doesn't support ray tracing in Wolfenstein so it's, standard mode here. It offers a decent lead over the RTX 3060 Ti on the minimum 99th percentile, but the average frame rate was a little disappointing, slipping behind the Nvidia card while overall performance was a long way off the RTX 3070.
Below we're running at 1440p and again the average frame rate is a little slow, while the minimum 99th percentile sits roughly equal between it, the RTX 3060 Ti and RTX 3070.
4K saw the minimum 99th and average frame rates compressed further still and no real gains made on the RTX 3070, although the 99th percentile was significantly higher than the RTX 3060 Ti, even if the average was a little slower.
Tomb Raider was far kinder to AMD, with the RX 6700 XT sitting some distance behind the RX 6800, but with slightly better performance than the RTX 3070. Job done, at least at these settings.
1440p was a similar story - the RX 6800 offers a lot higher performance, but the RX 6700 XT has the measure of the more expensive RTX 3070.
Switching up to 4K and things take a bit of a plunge with the RX 6700 XT only able to match the RTX 3060 Ti. I re-ran these numbers and got the same result.
Enabling DLSS and ray tracing and it's clear why AMD needs some alternative to DLSS, especially when it comes to adding performance to cards that would struggle at higher resolutions anyway. The RTX 3060 Ti managed a reasonably smooth 34fps minimum 99th percentile thanks to DLSS but the RX 6700 XT manages 19fps.
Smart Access Memory is a new feature from AMD that can enhance performance if you  have a Ryzen 5000 or select Ryzen 3000-series CPU, 500-series motherboard and RX 6000-series GPU. Clearly a niche setup, especially as buying these CPUs and graphics cards in the last few months has been extremely difficult. However, I've carried out performance in two games below. I'd liked to have done more but shipping-related gremlins and other factors meant I didn't have time. I'll revisit with more games in the near future. My test system differs from the one listed above and includes an AMD Ryzen 7 5800X and MSI MPG B550 Gaming Carbon WiFi, but the rest of the hardware is the same.
Dirt 5 saw a definite uptick in performance, although this only edged it a little closer to the RTX 3070 rather than leapfrogging it. The standout result is the minimum 99th percentile leaping from 78fps to 81fps at 1440 while the average rose 5fps from 90fps to 95fps.
Watch Dogs: Legion is relatively new and for now, ray tracing is disabled as it caused the benchmark to crash, so it's standard ultra settings in DX12. There was barely any difference between SAM mode and normal, though, but honestly didn't know what to expect as I haven't seen any other SAM benchmarks for this game.
The RTX 6700 XT mostly slots in nicely between the RTX 3060 Ti and RTX 3070, but just where it does depends on the game, whether DLSS or ray tracing are enabled and indeed whether you have a system capable of using Smart Access Memory. There are plenty more results out there from my fellow reviewers that point to decent gains with SAM enabled, but it's still a niche feature. In future I'll be including it more tests though, just as I do with DLSS. The cooler is very attractive, well made and super-quiet in games too. Absolutely no complaints from me here.
There were plenty of areas where the RX 6700 XT offers a compelling alternative to the RTX 3070 given its cheaper price and this is often at 1440p, which is the resolution AMD has been focussing on in press materials too. However, there are lots of instances where the RTX 3070 is a better buy and even the RTX 3060 Ti too, although the latter is arguably much better value than the RTX 3070 as well.
Even if the RX 6700 XT slots neatly into your budget and gaming requirements, which is entirely possible for a lot of people, the biggest issue is availability. Mining performance sits around 45MH/s with Ethereum and tuning the card can easily get down to sub 120W levels, which means that it will ultimately be attractive to miners. I've not seen a single RX 6000-series card available here in the UK for weeks, if not months, so a recommendation would start with being able to buy it, and at a fair price, and it remains to be seen whether that will be the case.
